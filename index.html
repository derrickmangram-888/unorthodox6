## Chapter #1:

Stop Chasing Money — Start Attracting It Through Alignment ##

Chasing money is the fastest way to run in place.
 Attracting money is the fastest way to accelerate.

Most people pursue money with a desperate energy:

“Please come to me.”

“Please let this work.”

“Please give me enough.”

But money responds the same way people do — if it feels chased, pressured, or begged for, it pulls away.

If it feels valued and respected, it leans in.

The Unorthodox Shift:

Instead of thinking, “How do I get money?” you switch to…
 “How can I align my thoughts, behaviors, and identity with the type of person money naturally flows to?”

Money flows to those who:

* Solve meaningful problems

* Believe they are worthy of earning

* Express gratitude for what they have

* Move with confidence, not desperation

* See opportunities, not obstacles

Real-Life Story:

The Designer Who Stopped Chasing Clients

Maria, a web designer from Atlanta, spent years in survival mode. Her strategy was simple:

chase as many clients as possible.

She lowered her prices, worked long nights, and begged people to hire her.

She was exhausted and broke.

Everything changed when she adopted one sentence from a mentor:
 “I choose to attract—not chase.”

She changed her pricing, redefined her boundaries, posted confidently about her expertise, and stopped taking “low-energy” clients.

Within 90 days, her income tripled. Not because she worked more—but because she became aligned with a higher version of herself.


## Chapter #2.

 Treat Money Like a Relationship — Not a Transaction ##


Most people treat money like a tool.

Prosperous people treat money like a relationship.

When you expect money to show up while ignoring it, resenting it, or mismanaging it, the relationship weakens.

Money wants appreciation, purpose, direction, and clarity.

The Unorthodox Practice:

Speak to your money like you would to a business partner:

“Thank you for all you provide.”

“Let’s grow together.”

“I trust you, and I manage you well.”

This might feel strange at first—but it builds the emotional connection most people lack.

Money is energy, and energy responds to intention.

Real-Life Story:

 The Entrepreneur Who Repaired Her Relationship With Money

Angela, a boutique owner, was terrified of looking at her finances. Every bill brought anxiety.

Every low month felt like a personal failure.

She avoided her accounting software the way people avoid the dentist.

A coach told her:

“Money is not judging you.

It’s waiting for you.”

She started a simple daily ritual:

five minutes of gratitude and acknowledgment every morning as she looked over her numbers.

In six months, her cashflow improved, her stress dropped, and she made better decisions because she finally saw what was really happening.

Money responds when you stop running from it.

## Chapter #3:

 Practice “Energetic Spending”:

 Spending With Intention, Not Fear ##


Most financial advice says:

“Spend less.

Save more."

But this advice alone keeps people trapped in fear.

Energetic spending is different.

It means making purchases from a place of:

* Confidence

* Alignment

*Expansion

* Vision

Instead of:

a) Guilt

b) Scarcity

c) Fear

d) Self-punishment

Money spent in fear shrinks.

Money spent in alignment expands.


A Real Example:

 The Consultant Who Invested Before He Felt Ready

Terrence, a consultant, wanted to scale his business.

He found a $6,000 program that could help him—but the price scared him.

His instinct was to hold on to the money “just in case.”

Then he asked himself an empowering question:

“Which decision aligns with the person I’m becoming?”

He invested.

Two months later, he signed a $25,000 contract using strategies from the program.

Energetic spending doesn’t mean being reckless — it means making

money decisions from your higher self, not your fearful self.

## Chapter #4:

Income Before Outcome:

Become the Person Who Can Hold Wealth ##


Most people want more money without preparing themselves to handle it.

But the universe does not give you what you want — it gives you what you can hold.

This is where identity comes in.

Unorthodox Practice:

When you want more money, ask:

“Who is the version of me that effortlessly earns this?”

Then, start embodying that person now:

* Speak like them

* Think like them

* Work like them

* Make decisions like them

* Carry yourself like them

Real-Life Story:

The Realtor Who Acted Like a Million-Dollar Agent


Before she ever earned six figures, realtor Jasmine began acting like a million-dollar agent:

She upgraded her wardrobe.

She improved her branding.

She walked into meetings with calm confidence.

She spoke with authority.

She treated every client like they were worth millions.

Within a year, she hit her first $500K in income.

She became wealth long before she earned it.

## Chapter #5:

Create Money Rituals That Anchor Prosperity ##


Rituals sound mystical, but they’re simply habits + intention.

Prosperous people have rituals that reinforce their wealth mindset:

a) Daily wealth affirmations

b) Weekly “money dates”

c) Journaling about gratitude

d) Visualizing financial success

e) Making decisions aligned with abundance

f) Reviewing finances with confidence

These rituals transform money from something stressful into something sacred.

Unorthodox Ritual Ideas:

Light a candle before you review your finances to create a calm atmosphere

Play empowering music while doing money tasks

Speak your goals out loud every morning

Write down “money wins” daily, no matter how small

Keep crisp $100 bills in your wallet to symbolize abundance

Real-Life Story:

 The Chef Who Used a Money Ritual to Escape Scarcity


Andre, a catering business owner, grew up with financial struggle.

Money was always a source of fear.

He started a simple nighttime ritual:

writing down three wealth-aligned actions he took that day — even tiny ones.

Over time, he began to see himself as someone who was in control.

In one year, he grew his monthly revenue from $4,000 to $18,000.

It started with a ritual that shifted how he saw himself.


## Chapter #6:

Use “Future Memory” Visualization:

Live From the Result, Not Toward It ##


Most people visualize goals they hope will happen.

High-level entrepreneurs visualize goals that have already happened.

This is called future memory — mentally stepping into the future version of yourself and experiencing it as if it is happening now.

Your subconscious begins shifting your behaviors automatically.

Ask yourself:

* What does wealthy me believe today?

* How does wealthy me solve problems?

* What risks does wealthy me take?

* How does wealthy me spend money?

*How does wealthy me move?

* Speak?

* Decide?

Real-Life Story:

The Business Owner Who Rehearsed His Success

Raymond, a gym owner, struggled for years to get 100 members.

He visualized his gym being full, his members smiling, the energy buzzing, and his bank account stable.

He didn’t visualize wanting it—he visualized living it.

Within months, he hit 120 members and eventually tripled his business.

Visualization didn’t bring success alone — but it brought the identity that created success.
